Critical Thinking

Something in the news that I have thought deeply about would be the issue of illegal immigration.

  • What is illegal immigration?

Illegal immigration is the process of entering a country to live there without following the immigration laws set in place by that country.

  • Why do people illegally immigrate?

There are many reasons a person might immigrate illegally. One is that the process of immigrating legally is too long or difficult for them. Another reason is they are just seeking a better life and more opportunity. A person also might illegally immigrate to escape persecution or oppression.

  • How do people illegally immigrate?

One way of illegally immigrating is crossing the border. This can be achieved by being smuggled on boats or crossing a physical border. Another way is to arrive in a country legally by obtaining a visa but not leaving when it expires. Sham marriages are another way of illegally immigrating an consist of marrying a person only to gain legal residency status.

  • Describe the problems faced by illegal immigrants in their new country.

Illegal immigrants usually have limited or no access to public services such as health care and education. They are also subject to exploitaion of their labor as employers are not required to follow employment laws. As a result they often make less than minimum wage and have unsafe working condition.

  • List the number of illegal immigrants in the US currently and recent policies that have been put in place to deter further illegal immigration.

According the Pew Research Center there are around 11.3 million people currently living in the US illegally. In 2002 the Enhanced Border Security and Visa Entry Reform Act provided more Border Patrol agents and required schools to report foreign students. In 2005 the REAL ID Act required the use of IDs in order to increase security by requiring them to enter government building, open bank accounts, and board planes.

Mindset

  • Provide an event and/or an example in your life when you operated from a 'Fixed' mindset.

A time I operated from a fixed mindset was when I tried to learn how to play poker but I lost all of my chips twice so I gave up trying to get better at it.

  • Provide an event and/or an example in your life when you operated from a 'Growth' mindset.

A time in my life I operated from a growth mindset would be in high school when I was struggling with calculus so I started attending the morning tutoring provided by the teacher in order to better understand the material.

  • Provide an example of how you could apply a 'Growth Mindset' in another classs you are currently taking this semester.

I could apply a growth mindset in my physics class by going to every class and trying to do all the homework instead of just trying to get by on the bare minimum.

  • Respond to the following prompt by providing examples of growth and fixed mindset response.
"Sam is a first-generation-to-college student whose career goal is to become an engineer. During high school Sam struggled in his math and science courses. Sam is currently in his freshman year and has completed the first 2 of 4 tests in science with grades of 50 and 60."

A fixed mindset response from Sam would be saying I struggled at science in high school and I am struggling now so I will never get better at it. A growth mindset from Sam would be realizing that just because I struggled at science in high school does not mean I can't get better. So in college I will use all the resources available to me such as tutoring and group study sessions in order get better at science.
